Spot our beautiful Tiger!



Scientific Name: Panthera tigris tigris

Diet: Carnivore

Size: Head to body: 5-6 ft; Tail : 2-3 ft

Weight: 240-500 lbs

Most Bengal tigers live in India and parts of southeast Asia. They live apart from each other and mark their territory by scratching the trees or peeing. Bengal tigers like to swim and enjoy the water. A female tiger can give birth to a litter of 3 to 4 cubs which she will then take care of for about a year and a half. Tigers will generally hunt alone such as hunting deer or antelop in the dark. A tiger stripes is unique to each tiger. Many tigers have been over hunted for their fur as well as other body parts that many people use in traditional medicines. Humans developing land for reasons such as farming or logging has also impacted the habitats of tigers.
