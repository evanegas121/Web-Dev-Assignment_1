Meet our Great White Shark!



Scientific Name: Carcharodon carcharias

Diet: Carnivore

Size: 15 ft - 20+ ft

Weight: 12.5+ tons

Habitat: Ocean

The Great White Shark can be found in the east and west coasts of North America, the south of Africa and southwest Australia. They can give birth to up to a dozen pups which then immediately swim after as the mother may attack them. Before adulthood pups will have to be careful o bigger predatore including other sharks, baby sharks will not survive the first year. Baby Shark eat fish while adult sharks tend to hunt sea mammals. They don't chew their food but instead rip of chunks and swallow them whole.
